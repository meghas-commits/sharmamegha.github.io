I work at the interface of operations management, analytics, and project execution. My academic and professional interests center on how organizations make better decisions, improve execution, and build capability in complex operating environments.

As a professor, I teach and write in areas related to operations, data science, and project management. Alongside my academic work, I am actively involved in executive education, where I engage with managers and leadership teams through training programs, workshops, and invited sessions.

My work seeks to combine analytical rigor with practical relevance. Across both classroom and executive settings, I focus on structured thinking, evidence-based decision-making, and the application of ideas to real organizational challenges.

This website brings together information on my research, teaching, executive education, talks, and professional activities.
